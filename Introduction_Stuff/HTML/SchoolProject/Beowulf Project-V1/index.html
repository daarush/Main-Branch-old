A Brief Introduction:

All of these poems (such as Beowulf) come from the period of Anglo-Saxons. The Anglo-Saxons came to England around 400 A.D. As the Roman Britiania was breaking up, the Germanic-speaking tribes began to inhibit the island. The main Germanic-speaking tribes were the Angles, Saxons, and the Jutes.

Characteristics of Anglo-Saxon's Poems:

  • Heroic poetry elements.
  • Christian ideals
  • Metaphors
  • Alliteration

Beowulf

First page of Beowulf in Cotton Vitellius A. xv
Beowulf is an Old English epic poem consisting of 3,182 alliterative lines. It is considered as one of the most important works of Anglo-Saxon literature.

Plot:

King Hrothgar rules a successful and prosperous kingdom. He builds a mead-hall called Heorot to celebrate and have a wonderful time. This angers a demon, Grendel, who lives in a swamp; so he terrorizes the Danes every night for twelve years. Eventually, a young hero named Beowulf comes to help the Danes and is eager to fight the demon. Since Hrothgar did a favor to Beowulf's father, therefore, accepts Beowulf. The Geatish warriors are invited to the Feast since Hrothgar accepts Beowulf to help the Danes. During the feast, a jealous Dane - Unferth - taunts Beowulf. When Grendel arrives, Beowulf proves to be stronger than Grendel and rips his arm; Grendel wounded runs away and dies in the swamp. The arm is hung to symbolize victory.

Little do the Danes and the successful Geatish warriors know, a new threat arrives - Grendel's mother. Grendel’s mother kills Aeschere and in return Beowulf sets out to kill Grendel’s mother. When Grenel’s mother and Beowulf meet, they fight underwater; they are equal until Beowulf sees a sword and swings it. Grendel's mother is killed and Beowulf decapitates the head of Grendel and brings it back to the Danes to celebrate.

Beowulf succeeds the throne of Geats after Hygelac’s son dies. He ruled wisely for 50 years; unfortunately a thief disturbs the dragon by taking the treasure he was guarding. The dragon lays destruction and Beowulf (with a bad of courageous warriors) fights the dragon, which is the last part of the poem. With help of Wiglaf, Beowulf kills the dragon but he also gets bitten by the dragon. Beowulf dies due to the poison (when the dragon bit him). Beowulf wished that his body should be buried with the treasure; according to the wish he is buried with the treasure.

Main Characters:
Beowulf
He is the epic 's protagonist; Beowulf is a Geatish hero who battles against the monster Grendel, the mother of Grendel, and the fire-breathing dragon.
King Hrothgar
He is the king of the Danes. Hrothgar enjoyed prosperity and success until Grendel terrorizes his regime.
Grendel
A demon who descended from Cain; who terrorizes Heorot and the domain of King Hrothgar due to his loneliness.
Grendel's Mother
A demon who lives in a cursed swamp; who terrorizes Heorot for revenge since Beowulf killed Grendel.
The dragon
An ancient, powerful serpent/dragon, who guards a horde of treasure in a hidden mound. Beowulf’s fight with the dragon takes up the third and final part of the epic.


Other Characters:
Unferth
A Dane who cannot (or willingly) fight and therefore, taunts Beowulf to prove him unworthy.
Wiglaf
A humble and young warrior who follows the Heroic code better than Beowulf’s kinsmens when fighting the dragon. He also proves himself as a successor to Beowulf.
Beow
The second king in the lineage of Danish rulers where the poem begins. He is known to be wise and admired; also is known to be admired as a leader.

Archetypes and Hero's Journey

-Archetypes are a repetition of patterns that is observed in many cultures.

Hover over each one to revel the text clearly and follow Hero's Journey.

Departure

Part 1

Ordinary World

Before the Hero begins a quest, where we learn about the character; we can see this is a safe place.

Call to Adventure

When the Hero or hero’s family is threatened or the safety is threatened; this is a call to adventure.

Refusal of the Call

Hero doesn’t want to leave his safe place to take on the quest

Supernatural Aid

The hero receives help from an supernatural who helps in on his journey

Crossing the first threshold

This is when the hero begins his adventure; he steps out of the safe zone

Belly of the Whale

The final separation from the hero's known world and self.

Initiation

Part 2

Road of Trials

These trials will test the Hero where he will go transformations

Meeting with the Goddess

Supernatural aid from a goddess that can help the Hero.

Temptation away from the true path.

Many temptations occur to stray from the quest; can be physical or physiological.

Atonement with the father

The hero faces his enemy or someone with great power, this is usually the center point of the journey.

Apotheosis (becoming god-like)

Greater understanding is achieved.

The Ultimate Boon

The completion of the quest.

Return

Part 3

Refusal to Return

The hero may not want to return to the ordinary world.

Magic Flight

The return can be dangerous as the starting of the quest.

Rescue from without

The hero needs help to return back to the natural world.

Crossing of the Return Threshold

The hero needs to incorporate the wisdom (he gained from the journey) into his normal world.

Master of Two Worlds

The hero achieves balance between the ordinary and his new world.

Wergild:

Literally means “body price”; the amount of compensation paid for an offense to the injured party or, in case of death, to his family. This can be a loop until someone pays the price. We see this in Beowulf because Grendel started killing humans and did not pay for the body price; then we see Beowulf killing Grendel and not paying the price for Grendel’s mother; finally we Grenel’s mother kills Aeschere and in return Beowulf kills Grendel’s mother. This is a loop because the family of Grendel and Horthgar are fighting; nobody paid the price.


Wyrd:

Means fate; Beowulf knew that he was going to be killed by the dragon, in the final part of the poem, and yet he still battled.


Heorot:

A mead hall, built by King Horthgar; the poem Beowulf was focused around the Heorot.
