Beowulf
Beowulf is an Old English epic poem consisting of 3,182 alliterative lines. It is considered as one of the most important works of Anglo-Saxon literature.

Plot:

King Hrothgar rules a successful and prosperous kingdom. He builds a mead-hall called Heorot to celebrate and have a wonderful time. This angers a demon, Grendel, who lives in a swamp; so he terrorizes the Danes every night for twelve years. Eventually, a young hero named Beowulf comes to help the Danes and is eager to fight the demon. Since Hrothgar did a favor to Beowulf's father, therefore, accepts Beowulf. The Geatish warriors are invited to the Feast since Hrothgar accepts Beowulf to help the Danes. During the feast, a jealous Dane - Unferth - taunts Beowulf. When Grendel arrives, Beowulf proves to be stronger than Grendel and rips his arm; Grendel wounded runs away and dies in the swamp. The arm is hung to symbolize victory.

Little do the Danes and the successful Geatish warriors know, a new threat arrives - Grendel's mother. Grendel’s mother kills Aeschere and in return Beowulf sets out to kill Grendel’s mother. When Grenel’s mother and Beowulf meet, they fight underwater; they are equal until Beowulf sees a sword and swings it. Grendel's mother is killed and Beowulf decapitates the head of Grendel and brings it back to the Danes to celebrate.

Beowulf succeeds the throne of Geats after Hygelac’s son dies. He ruled wisely for 50 years; unfortunately a thief disturbs the dragon by taking the treasure he was guarding. The dragon lays destruction and Beowulf (with a bad of courageous warriors) fights the dragon, which is the last part of the poem. With help of Wiglaf, Beowulf kills the dragon but he also gets bitten by the dragon. Beowulf dies due to the poison (when the dragon bit him). Beowulf wished that his body should be buried with the treasure; according to the wish he is buried with the treasure.

Main Characters:
Beowulf
He is the epic 's protagonist; Beowulf is a Geatish hero who battles against the monster Grendel, the mother of Grendel, and the fire-breathing dragon.
King Hrothgar
He is the king of the Danes. Hrothgar enjoyed prosperity and success until Grendel terrorizes his regime.
Grendel
A demon who descended from Cain; who terrorizes Heorot and the domain of King Hrothgar due to his loneliness.
Grendel's Mother
A demon who lives in a cursed swamp; who terrorizes Heorot for revenge since Beowulf killed Grendel.
The dragon
An ancient, powerful serpent/dragon, who guards a horde of treasure in a hidden mound. Beowulf’s fight with the dragon takes up the third and final part of the epic.


Other Characters:
Unferth
A Dane who cannot (or willingly) fight and therefore, taunts Beowulf to prove him unworthy.
Wiglaf
A humble and young warrior who follows the Heroic code better than Beowulf’s kinsmens when fighting the dragon. He also proves himself as a successor to Beowulf.
Beow
The second king in the lineage of Danish rulers where the poem begins. He is known to be wise and admired; also is known to be admired as a leader.
