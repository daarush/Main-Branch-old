Vocabulary

Wergild:

Literally means “body price”; the amount of compensation paid for an offense to the injured party or, in case of death, to his family. This can be a loop until someone pays the price. We see this in Beowulf because Grendel started killing humans and did not pay for the body price; then we see Beowulf killing Grendel and not paying the price for Grendel’s mother; finally we Grenel’s mother kills Aeschere and in return Beowulf kills Grendel’s mother. This is a loop because the family of Grendel and Horthgar are fighting; nobody paid the price.


Wyrd:

Means fate; Beowulf knew that he was going to be killed by the dragon, in the final part of the poem, and yet he still battled.


Heorot:

A mead hall, built by King Horthgar; the poem Beowulf was focused around the Heorot.
