Archetypes and Hero's Journey

-Archetypes are a repetition of patterns that is observed in many cultures.

Hover over each one to revel the text clearly and follow Hero's Journey.

Departure

Part 1

Ordinary World

Before the Hero begins a quest, where we learn about the character; we can see this is a safe place.

Call to Adventure

When the Hero or hero’s family is threatened or the safety is threatened; this is a call to adventure.

Refusal of the Call

Hero doesn’t want to leave his safe place to take on the quest

Supernatural Aid

The hero receives help from an supernatural who helps in on his journey

Crossing the first threshold

This is when the hero begins his adventure; he steps out of the safe zone

Belly of the Whale

The final separation from the hero's known world and self.

Initiation

Part 2

Road of Trials

These trials will test the Hero where he will go transformations

Meeting with the Goddess

Supernatural aid from a goddess that can help the Hero.

Temptation away from the true path.

Many temptations occur to stray from the quest; can be physical or physiological.

Atonement with the father

The hero faces his enemy or someone with great power, this is usually the center point of the journey.

Apotheosis (becoming god-like)

Greater understanding is achieved.

The Ultimate Boon

The completion of the quest.

Return

Part 3

Refusal to Return

The hero may not want to return to the ordinary world.

Magic Flight

The return can be dangerous as the starting of the quest.

Rescue from without

The hero needs help to return back to the natural world.

Crossing of the Return Threshold

The hero needs to incorporate the wisdom (he gained from the journey) into his normal world.

Master of Two Worlds

The hero achieves balance between the ordinary and his new world.
